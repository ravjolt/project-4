Heart Analysis

Project 4

Ed, Raven, Ramsha, Yen, Jonathan

Objective

Use machine learning and various other libraries to analyze patient data, finding important trends and visualizations regarding health status

Our group found an online dataset with over 400 samples with regards to the status of their health, specifically in regards to their heart. We looked into the initial CSV file, and had to clean this initial data in order to analyze properly. Using pandas, Catboost, Scikit-learn, and other dependencies, we were able to turn our simple CSV file into a complex machine model, predicting whether or not a patient was likely or not to have a heart attack. Important findings had to deal with gender, types of chest pain, and cholesterol levels in regards to our model predicting the likelihood of potential heart disease. Ed in particular did an excellent job manipulating our data and building multiple machine models that were trained and tested to produce valuable outputs.

Prediction

Creating a Heart Disease Prediction web page based on our machine modeling

Using Heroku to deploy our app to the web, our group used machine modeling and data analysis to create an input page where our model could determine whether or not someone is likely to have a heart disease, based on the health data entered. Using various categories from our original data including age, sex, chest pain, blood pressure, cholesterol, among others, a prediction would be found based upon results from our analysis of prior patients. Yen built the page and the application route necessary to obtain this prediction page, and this is not a clinically proven study. From a basic CSV file, we were able to transform a spreadsheet into an intelligent computer prediction of heart disease. Oh the power of data science!
